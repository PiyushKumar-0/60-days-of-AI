## SECTION 12: Interview Talking Points

**Most Impressive Achievements to Lead With**
1. Built and shipped **5 distinct end-to-end projects** spanning full-stack, ML, and computer vision — not tutorials, but applications with real logic (auth, prediction, real-time detection)
2. Completed **JP Morgan and Deloitte job simulations** — concrete, verifiable exposure to enterprise engineering and security practices
3. Maintained an **8.66 CGPA** while building a substantial independent project portfolio in parallel

**Key Stories to Discuss (STAR-ready)**
- **AI Resume Analyzer**: Talk through the technical challenge of matching resume content against job descriptions and generating a meaningful ATS score — what logic you used, what was hard about scoring "fit" objectively, and how you validated it worked.
- **Face Detection System**: Discuss the real-time performance challenge — getting OpenCV to process live webcam feeds smoothly, handling bounding boxes and detection statistics, and any tradeoffs between accuracy and speed.
- **E-Commerce Application**: Be ready to explain your authentication approach, how the cart/database interaction was structured, and any specific bug or design decision you had to work through.
- **Bengaluru House Price Predictor**: Explain your data preprocessing steps, which features mattered most for prediction accuracy, and how you evaluated the model.

**Areas Recruiters/Interviewers Are Likely to Probe**
- **"Walk me through your DSA problem-solving process"** — be ready with specific examples, not just "I practiced recursion and arrays." Have 2–3 problems you can whiteboard confidently.
- **"How did you deploy/host this project?"** — if not yet deployed, be honest and proactive: "It currently runs locally; I'm in the process of deploying it to [platform]" shows self-awareness rather than a gap.
- **"Did you work on this alone or with a team?"** — since all listed projects appear individual, be ready to speak to how you'd collaborate in a team setting, and mention any group coursework or informal collaboration.
- **"What would you improve about [project] if you had more time?"** — interviewers use this to test self-awareness and technical depth; prepare a genuine answer for each of your 5 projects.
- **"Why full-stack AND ML — what's the direction you actually want?"** — be ready with a clear, confident answer on which direction excites you more, even if you're open to either. Recruiters read "I do everything" as unfocused if you can't articulate a preference.
